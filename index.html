Queues

A queue is a data structure that follows the First In, First Out (FIFO) principle. Meaning that the first element added to the queue is the first one to be removed. Think of it like a queue of people waiting in line, where the person who arrives first is the first to be served.

The two main operations associated with a queue are:

  1. Enqueue: Adds an element to the (end) of the queue
  2. Dequeue: Removes and returns the element from the front (beginning) of the queue.

Additionally, there is often a third operation:

  1. Front (or Peek): Returns the element at the front of the queue without removing it.
